Your Role as a Data Collector

Data Collection is the backbone of everything we do at PFF Enterprise. As a Data Collector, you will play a key role in delivering player and play-level data to our team clients.

This is not a full-time job, but it can serve as a great way to supplement your income while gaining hands-on experience in football data analysis. If you're passionate about the game and eager to get a foot in the door of the football industry, this role offers a valuable opportunity.

While there is no guarantee of a full-time role, every full-time football data analyst at PFF started as a part-time contractor. Each year, new full-time analysts are hired exclusively from within our part-time team.

Remote position — no relocation required.

Work centred around the 2026 NCAA College Football season (late August through early December).

⚠ Training requirement: You must successfully complete the PFF Enterprise Academy — a five-week training program — before working in-season. Week one is an unpaid e-learning program (PFFU). Training games are paid from week two onwards.
💰

What We Are Looking For

  • A deep passion for football and a strong understanding of the game
  • Familiarity with PFF Enterprise and football data collection, or a willingness to learn
  • Strong communication skills, both written and verbal
  • Exceptional attention to detail and the ability to meet tight deadlines
  • Great time management skills paired with a positive work ethic
  • Availability to commit at least 6 hours most Saturdays during the football season

Training & Evaluation

Once you've completed the full application and been accepted into our training program, you'll begin the PFF Enterprise Academy — a five-week training and evaluation period starting June 7.

Week one is PFFU, an unpaid e-learning program that must be completed before your first training game is assigned. From week two onwards, training games are paid. The Academy requires around 10 hours of your time each week and is self-paced from home, with some mandatory structured drop-in sessions each week. More details will be provided to successful applicants before the start of training.

PFF Enterprise operates as a meritocracy — only those who excel in the Academy will earn a place on the team for in-season work and beyond.

In-Season Commitments

The Saturday commitment is non-negotiable.

You'll need to commit at least six hours most Saturdays during the NCAA season. If you're unable or unwilling to dedicate six hours on Saturdays, this role might not be the right fit for you.

With practice your efficiency will improve — many first-year collectors find themselves wanting to take on additional Saturday and Sunday games as the season progresses.
